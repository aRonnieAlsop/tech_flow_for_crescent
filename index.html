Guiding Principle

Every tool should support a low-staff, guest-friendly hotel operation. If a product does not connect cleanly to Mews, reduce manual work, and fit the opening-day guest journey, it should not be added yet.

Purpose: finish the minimum Mews setup required for rooms to be bookable, paid for, checked into, and checked out of.

Purpose: make sure the guest can move from booking to room access with as little staff help as possible.

Purpose: research and decide how rates will update across Wix, Expedia, Booking.com, and other channels.

Purpose: prepare the physical and integration pieces that make low-staff operation possible.

Core Flow

Opening Phase

Direct Booking

Guest books through the Wix website using the Mews booking engine.

OTA Booking

Guest books through Expedia, Booking.com, Hotels.com, or another connected channel.

Walk-Up Guest

Guest arrives without a reservation and uses the iPad kiosk to check availability.

↓

Mews PMS

The central system for rooms, rates, availability, reservations, guest profiles, check-in status, and checkout status.

↓

Wix Booking Engine

Displays live availability and rates from Mews.

Square Payments

Handles deposits, balances, and walk-in payments.

RMS

Revenue tool to be selected. May automate rate changes if practical for Phase 1.

Channel Manager

Keeps OTA availability and rates synchronized with Mews.

↓

Remote Check-In

Guest completes check-in before arrival when available.

iPad Kiosk Check-In

Guest completes check-in on-site if they did not finish before arrival, or if they are walking up.

↓

Identity Verification

May include license scan, selfie match, or reservation detail confirmation.

Registration Card

Guest signs required digital registration paperwork.

Payment Verified

Payment method, deposit, or remaining balance is confirmed.

↓

Guest Middleware

Middleware to be selected. Must support remote check-in, kiosk flow, Mews, and Onity DirectKey.

Onity DirectKey

Issues the mobile room key once the guest is approved and checked in.

↓

Guest Unlocks Room

The ideal experience: guest books, pays, verifies, receives a mobile key, and enters the room with little or no staff involvement.

Fallback Path

Mobile Key Issue

Guest phone, Bluetooth, app, or verification process fails.

Store / Staff Support

Guest goes to the store or contacts staff for help.

Physical Key

Staff uses primary or backup laptop to encode a card or fob.
